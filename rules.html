Chess Rules Summary

Basic Rules

  • Objective: The goal is to checkmate the opponent’s king.
  • Illegal Moves: You cannot move into check, leave your king in check, or capture the opponent’s king.
  • Castling: Castling is legal only if neither the king nor rook involved has moved, and no squares the king passes through are under attack.
  • En Passant: A pawn may capture another pawn en passant only on the move immediately following the opponent’s two-square pawn advance.
  • Promotion: A pawn reaching the eighth rank must be promoted to a queen, rook, bishop, or knight (Article 3.7e).

Clock & Timing

  • Clock Use: A move is only completed when a player stops their own clock and starts the opponent’s.
  • Time Control: Games are lost on time if the required number of moves isn’t made in the allotted period—unless checkmate is impossible.
  • Default Time: Players arriving late may forfeit the game, often after 0 minutes unless the competition rules specify otherwise.

Recording Moves

  • Scoresheet: Players must record their moves using algebraic notation unless they have less than 5 minutes and no increment.
  • Draw Offers: Must be recorded on the scoresheet using a symbol like "(=)".

Draw Conditions

  • Stalemate: Occurs when a player has no legal move and is not in check.
  • Threefold Repetition: Players may claim a draw if the same position occurs three times.
  • 50-Move Rule: A draw can be claimed if no pawn move or capture has occurred in 50 consecutive moves.

Irregularities & Penalties

  • Illegal Move: Must be corrected; the opponent may gain extra time.
  • Third Illegal Move: Results in a loss unless checkmate is impossible.
  • Electronic Devices: If a phone rings, the player loses the game.
  • Misconduct: Disruptive behavior or cheating can result in loss or expulsion.
